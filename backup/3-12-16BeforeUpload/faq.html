    What is FIRST?

    Every year, over 3,000 robotics teams from high schools all across the world each build a unique robot to compete in an annual competition, organized by FIRST ("For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology"). This competition is called FRC ("FIRST Robotics Competition") and begins early in January, when FIRST releases the game challenge that teams’ robots must perform. Following the release of the game, teams have just six weeks to design, fabricate, program, and test the robot to be ready to compete.

    FIRST is a large non-profit organization that holds robotics competitions for high school students throughout the United States and around the world. FIRST stands for “For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology” because its challenging competitions encourage students to innovate and develop their technical skills in a competitive, fun, and rewarding environment.

    Over 3,000 teams participated in the FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) this year, and that number is quickly growing because of the large number of parents, teachers, and students who realize what an outstanding learning opportunity this program provides. Each year a game is announced and teams are given roughly six weeks to design and build a robot that can compete effectively in that game. After the 6 weeks are over, the robot, whether it is finished or not, is put into a large bag that cannot be opened until competition time.

    In the process of making the robot, students learn essential STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math) skills, such as programming, electronics, CAD (Computer Aided Design), mechanics, and more. Students learn these skills from other experienced students and from adult mentors, many of whom are professionals in their fields. Students also learn critical teamwork and working skills, such as communicating effectively, meeting deadlines, and leadership skills. Participating in the robotics program nurtures and prepares students for both college and careers in modern technology.

    As well as as FRC teams, other FIRST teams include FTC (FIRST Tech Challenge) teams for 7-12 graders, and FLL (FIRST Lego League) teams for grades four to eight.

    To learn more, go to the FIRST webiste at www.firstinspires.org
